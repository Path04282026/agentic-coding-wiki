# Greg Brockman

**Role:** Co-founder & President of [OpenAI](references/entities/openai.md)
**Dimensions most associated with:** [01 - Agent Loop](references/dimensions/01-agent-loop.md), [03 - Security and Sandboxing](references/dimensions/03-security-and-sandboxing.md), [05 - Multi-Agent Architecture](references/dimensions/05-multi-agent-architecture.md), [09 - Philosophical Divergence](references/dimensions/09-philosophical-divergence.md)

## Key Contributions

- Articulated the "harness is your body, model is your brain" metaphor — the foundational Codex design philosophy
- Proposed the Intelligence × Convenience matrix for evaluating agent products
- Championed scalable oversight as a research priority dating to 2017
- Advocated for the internal-first approach: "If you can't make it useful for yourself, how are you going to make it useful for everyone else?"
- 2030 vision: material abundance + compute scarcity, with billions of agents needed

## Notable Quotes

> "The harness is your body and the model is your brain."

> "There's two dimensions to what makes an AI desirable. There's intelligence and then there's convenience."

> "Maybe instead of the user driving this thing, maybe let the model actually drive the interaction."

> "If you can't even make it useful for yourself, how are you going to make it extremely useful for everyone else?"

> "We will be in a world of material abundance… but it'll be a world of absolute compute scarcity."
