# Interview — OpenClaw — Peter Steinberger (2026-02-01)

**Format:** Podcast / live demo
**Speaker:** [Peter Steinberger](references/entities/peter-steinberger.md)
**Lines:** ~1,138

## Key Takeaways by Dimension

### [01 - Agent Loop](references/dimensions/01-agent-loop.md)
- Live demo of OpenClaw in action
- Bug fix workflow: screenshot of bug tweet → WhatsApp → agent reads tweet, checks out repo, fixes bug, commits, replies on Twitter
- "It's just like having a new weird friend that is also really smart and resourceful that lives on your computer"

### [02 - Tool Systems](references/dimensions/02-tool-systems.md)
- Image-based prompting: Peter uses screenshots extensively for context
- Multi-modal input: text, images, voice — all through messaging interface
- "Why should I use MyFitnessPal when I have an infinitely resourceful assistant?"

### [06 - UI and Distribution](references/dimensions/06-ui-and-distribution.md)
- WhatsApp works even on shaky internet (Morocco trip story)
- Agent as phone companion — checking restaurants, translating, finding places
- The mental model: "unshackled ChatGPT" with system access

## Notable Quotes

> "I was still in Morocco and someone sent me a tweet of a bug. I made a picture of the tweet, posted on WhatsApp. It read the tweet, understood the bug, checked out the repo, fixed it, committed, and replied to the person on Twitter."

> "This will blend away probably 80% of the apps that you have on your phone."

> "It's just like having a new weird friend that is also really smart and resourceful."
